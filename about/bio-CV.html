Bio

Lark Alder aka VCR works in video, installation, interactive media, critical design, and performance. VCR stands for Virtually Conflicted Reality. Their work queers possible applications of developing technology. Through speculative fiction and critical design, Lark leverages humor to tackle subjects as diverse as biosurveillance, social media addiction, trauma, rape culture, genomic modification, human-computer desire, and incantation in code.

Lark received an MFA from UC Berkeley and BA from Harvard University. They received grants from the Center for Cultural Innovation, East Bay Fund for Artists, and the Murphy & Cardogan Contemporary Art Awards. Their work has shown at Frameline Film Festival, MIX NYC, the San Francisco International Arts Festival, and Schuwels Museum in Berlin. A dedicated educator, Lark has taught classes at UC Berkeley, SAE Expression College, and is currently teaching digital media art at San Jose State University's CADRE Media Lab.
